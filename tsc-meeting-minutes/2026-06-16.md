## Meeting Summary \- Based on AI Summary

* **Upcoming Changes to TSC Governance – Proposal:**  
  * Claudia noted that the PGB is voting on governance language concerning the number of TSC chairs within the next few weeks. The proposal, initiated by David, Omar, and others, aims to increase the minimum number of co-chairs to three to distribute the workload more effectively The group plans to respond to the outcome of this vote once the results are finalized.  
* **ACTION Institute Collaboration Strategy:**   
  * J.R. led a discussion on recent interactions with the ACTION Institute, seeking feedback on how to formalize the engagement. Debadatta suggested utilizing the Institute's manpower and laboratory access to act as a "think tank" for experimentation. The consensus reached was that the group should identify "adjacencies" between the ACTION Institute's research and existing workstreams to focus the collaboration, rather than engaging at a high, non-specific level.  
  * To operationalize the engagement, Bill proposed assigning specific workstreams to track related ACTION Institute activities. J.R. clarified that the primary value of this collaboration is the "accuracy" of research directions and early access to academic thinking, rather than an expectation of increased speed in execution.  
  * Akila emphasized the importance of defining clear success criteria for the partnership to ensure the group receives value from the academic researchers while allowing them to leverage the community for dissemination. Akila and Josiah suggested that the group could treat specific research areas as "bets," using academic resources to conduct experiments that the industry is curious about, and evaluating the results quarterly.  
  * Salim raised concerns regarding intellectual property limitations, specifically regarding how to obtain early access to research before official white papers are published. J.R. acknowledged this concern, noting that the group needs to explore ways to operationalize early access while respecting the publication cycles of academic partners.  
* **Guidelines for Tool Adoption and Maintenance:**   
  * Daniel and David addressed the long-term support obligations for adopting tools. David clarified that CoSAI will establish a baseline requirement where any entity contributing a project or tool must provide ongoing support, as the organization cannot assume maintenance responsibilities for free. David and Omar are currently developing guidelines for evaluating future contributions, using lessons learned from previous donations.  
* **Agentic Identity Standards Proposal:**   
  * Daniel introduced a proposal from NVIDIA and another partner regarding a standard for identity delegation and revocation. David expressed strong support, noting that solving the identity problem is a high priority for CoSAI. The group discussed the risk of industry fragmentation if they do not provide an open, standardized approach, with Bill and Debadatta emphasizing the need for an open structure similar to DNS or Let's Encrypt to ensure interoperability.  
  * The group decided to present the identity standards proposal to WS4 to gauge interest and test the standard against broader enterprise needs. Claudia will coordinate with Sarah and Ian to get this item on the WS4 agenda, and interested TSC members are encouraged to attend that session to provide input.  
* **GitHub Organizational Architecture and README Standardization:**   
  * David provided an update on the GitHub-first architecture efforts, explaining that the focus is on standardizing READMEs and managing repositories to ensure data is captured effectively. David argued against creating "presentation layers" like GitHub pages at this stage, noting that the primary users are other workstream members who require structured data, discoverability, and automation, not web pages.  
* **Digital Signing and Schematized Data:**   
  * Daniel proposed that the group apply its signing standard to any skills or models published to demonstrate best practices, to which David agreed. Additionally, David outlined a plan to decompose narrative white papers into schematized, structured data, such as JSON or YAML, to enable automated analysis and reduce the reliance on narrative-heavy formats. David plans to expose this intermediate work product for the group to review and refine as a pattern for future contributions.
