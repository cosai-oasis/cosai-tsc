### Meeting Summary

* **Publication Status of MCP Security and CoSAI RSAC Presentations**: Kelly confirmed that she is still awaiting an official answer regarding the submission and publication of the MCP security presentation materials at RSAC. J.R. clarified that the request pertains to hosting the original RSAC presentations and the corresponding follow-up blog posts on the CoSAI site. Kelly committed to following up and obtaining a response within one or two more days.

* **MCP Security Blog Post Development**: Sarah announced there is a draft blog post, derived from the content of the MCP security presentation she gave with Jason, and that the post will be published with a link to the Agentic Identity paper. The intent is to have this material available to address questions arising from the MCP paper, specifically relating to identity.

* **Review of TSC Workstream Status and Leadership**: J.R. proposed reviewing each workstream to determine their focus, status, and, critically, to identify and discuss candidates for co-lead positions to address current leadership issues. The immediate goal is to establish a roster of candidates for discussion and decision-making within the TSC.

* **Workstream 1 Model Signing Maturity Levels Status**: Jay reported the deliverable is currently in the editing phase, working to shorten the very long document, ensure cohesiveness, and establish a single voice throughout. The paper is on track to be ready for review before Matt's upcoming departure for paternity leave.

* **Workstream 1 Co-Lead Identification and Commitment**: Following Andre's departure and anticipating Matt's paternity leave, WS1 is seeking one to two new co-leads to maintain continuity, as continuity is essential for the workstream. Although several people have raised their hands to contribute, Jay, the existing co-lead, is focused on identifying those willing to commit to the responsibility of leading, expecting a definitive answer within a week or two. 

  * Jay assured the TSC that they are prepared for the transition, with enough work, particularly on the editorial side of the paper, to occupy the workstream until new leadership is confirmed.

  * Once the maturity level paper is complete, WS1 plans to focus on model vulnerability, which has been gaining attention and may impact the conversation around agentic AI. Jay plans to leverage this work to inform other organizations and potentially provide a dimension to the CoSAI Risk Map (CoSAI-RM) by analyzing risks at different maturity levels.

* **TSC Process for Workstream Lead Approval**: J.R. inquired whether the Project Governing Board (PGB) must vote on the approval of new workstream leads, clarifying that this concerns a workstream, not a Special Interest Group (SIG). Kelly confirmed that the Technical Steering Committee (TSC) has the authority to appoint workstream leads via lazy consensus.

* **Workstream 2 Shared Responsibility Framework Review**: Josiah confirmed that the Shared Responsibility Framework is largely complete, with David L.’s comments and Bill’s answers addressed. J.R. proposed scheduling a follow-up meeting with Josiah, Akila, Bill, and Christopher to ensure the narrative and key takeaways are clean before bringing the paper forward for a vote, which is critical due to the topic's complexity involving various personas and platform vendors.

* **Proposed Rotation of Shared Responsibility SIG to Workstream 3**: Josiah discussed plans to rotate the governance-focused Shared Responsibility SIG, currently under Workstream 2, to Workstream 3 after the Shared Responsibility Framework is finalized. Josiah, Shrey, Christine, and Vinay are currently trying to coordinate a meeting to discuss the handoff and identification of new SIG leadership, with the understanding that the SIG's future work will be aligned with the AI security governance.

* **Workstream 2 Zero Trust Paper and Roadmap Development**: WS2 Zero Trust paper is nearing completion, needing only final cleanup and references before soliciting final reviewers. Josiah reiterated the need for a roadmap of future deliverables to help sequence work and manage community expectations, as previous delays were caused by prioritizing other deliverables.

* **Inclusion of  Karttik Panda in Workstream 3 and Related SIGs**: J.R. informed the Workstream 2 and 3 leads that Karthik from OpenAI, who has experience in Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) frameworks, is interested in participating in Workstream 3 and SecureCode SIG. J.R. encouraged the co-leads to involve  Karttik in their activities as the governance work transitions.

* **Workstream 3, SecureCode SIG Update and Next Steps**: WS3 is primarily focused on SIG 1: Security of AI-Assisted Code Development SIG (SecureCode) and CoSAI-RM. Aaron, SecureCode co-lead, reported that the SIG is currently engaged in numerous discussions and is focused on translating those discussions into tangible deliverables such as code guard rules or written guidance for AI-assisted implementation.

* **Finalization of WS3 SecureCode SIG Co-Leads**: J.R. emphasized the urgent need to finalize the co-leads for SecureCode. Aaron confirmed that Thomas, Ramraj, and himself (all from Cisco) are involved, but the workstream seeks a non-Cisco co-lead to bring a different perspective. Raghuram successfully recruited Asmae from Intel to volunteer as a co-lead for SecureCode SIG, providing the non-Cisco perspective requested.  Though a concern was raised as Asmae was also tapped to lead SIG 2: SecureADLC.  

* **CoSAI-RM V1 Release Dependency on Workstream Contributions**: David L. stated that, in his opinion, a Version 1 (V1) release of the CoSAI-RM is imminent but cannot be published until the unique control and risk elements identified in the existing papers from Workstreams 1, 2, and 4 are adequately represented in the CoSAI-RM. He emphasized that all workstream leads must connect with the WS3 leadership to ensure the controls are appropriately populated for the V1 release, which they hope to achieve by the beginning of May.

* **Proposal for Ecosystem Benchmarking Collaboration**: David L. suggested that the TSC should initiate a conversation around benchmarking skills and testing harnesses from a security standpoint with groups like Google, Cloud Security Alliance, OWASP, AI Alliance, and ML Commons. This initiative addresses a gap in evaluation given that CoSAI is not funded as a benchmarking group.

* **Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) Discussion in AI Ecosystems**: David L. highlighted a growing external pull from governments for guidance on PQC requirements within AI ecosystems, citing recent announcements by Google and Cloudflare. He proposed that the TSC consider making a statement or developing a follow-up document addressing PQC, particularly regarding transport security and its relevance to existing papers like the Agentic Identity and MCP papers.

* **Workstream 4 Next Steps and SecureADLC SIG Co-Lead Candidates**: Ian provided an update on the MCP Dev Summit, noting his talk on security plug-in infrastructure for agentic workflows but confirming there was no direct CoSAI presentation. Sarah reported that SIG 2: Security of Agent Development Lifecycle (SecureADLC) SIG has two internal co-leads who have volunteered (Parul and Kathleen from IBM/Red Hat), but they need a third non-IBM/Red Hat co-lead, and they need the TSC to approve the existing candidates. J.R. requested Sarah and Ian to finalize their recommendations for the SIG 2 leadership and forward the names to Akila and themself for discussion and voting in the next TSC meeting.

* **Workstream 4 SecureADLC SIG Content Prioritization**: Workstream 4 will initially focus on the data plane space, specifically the security controls that would be placed there, as this proposal has been approved. Ian explained that focusing on the data plane is a way to manage the scope, as the full Agent Development Life Cycle (ADLC) is too large to execute on meaningfully.
