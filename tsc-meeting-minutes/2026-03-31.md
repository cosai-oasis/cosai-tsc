### Meeting Summary

* **Welcome and New Member Introduction:**   
  * The group welcomed Karttik Panda as the new Technical Steering Committee (TSC) representative from OpenAI, who is a Technical Program Manager (TPM) within the OpenAI security team and leads the Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) engineering initiatives.  
* **RSA Conference Debrief:**  
  * PGB and TSC Working Session on Monday hosted by Cisco: J.R. noted the excellent arrangements made by Omar and his team. One key takeaway from Dinesh (Intel’s PGB rep) was the suggestion to test the CoSAI Risk Management (CoSAI-RM) framework within member enterprises to gather feedback and assess its traction in managing AI risk.  
  * *Contributor Recognition and Membership Activation:* A discussion took place regarding how to formally recognize contributors to the various work streams, with ideas ranging from a "CoSAI contributor of the month" award to providing feedback to their management. There was a significant focus on activating more members beyond the existing WS contributors to broaden skill sets and create a virtuous cycle where successful implementations within companies drive further contributions.  
  * *SIG 1 and SIG 2 Overlap:* The group discussed the confusion and overlap between the new SIG 1 (Agent-assisted Development) and SIG 2 (Agentic Lifecycle) descriptions, a concern initially raised by Nicholas Butts. Sarah, Shrey, and Ian, who were representatives at the meeting, committed to providing clearer articulation of the boundaries between the two SIGs, particularly concerning the life cycle development for agents versus the agentic workflow.  
  * *Itemizing Company Benefits:* Bill proposed creating a list of benefits for participating companies, noting that their research team did not clearly see the product or outcome of alliance relationships, particularly as the company grew. David agreed that this was a reasonable request to add to the evaluation list.   
  * *Invite-only Anthropic Breakfast Panel:* The Anthropic-hosted breakfast panel, moderated by Morgan from TR, was well-received and had a packed room. David and Sarah noted that they heard good feedback and observed posts about the event on social media from non-aligned individuals.  
  * *MCP Security Talk:* Sarah and Jason presented a talk on MCP Security to a full, wait-listed room. They covered the WS4 white paper, anchored the theory with anecdotes, and received more questions than time allowed. Sarah committed to sharing the presentation deck and speaking notes with Claudia.  
  * *Use Case Talk and Blog Post:* Akila and J.R’s. talk on use cases, which was one of the last sessions, had over 100 people and generated significant interest in CoSAI participation. The talk condensed guidance into three case studies on attacks and prescriptive calls to action for enterprises to improve their security posture. The speakers focused on framing the discussion in terms of use cases and integrating recent attention on various claws (e.g., Open Claw, Neo Claw) into the scenarios, which will be summarized in a blog post for TSC review and publication on the CoSAI website.  
  * *Marketing Strategy for New Collateral:* The group discussed coordinating the publication of several pieces of content, including the RSA talk summaries, the agentic futures scenario paper, and the agentic identity paper from WS4. Daniel suggested that when combining multiple topics for a marketing story, the marketing team should weave a narrative that connects the sub-pieces to prevent them from feeling like non sequiturs and to make the content more effective.   
  * *Booth Activity and Lead Follow-up*: Kelly provided an update on the OASIS booth, estimating they had nearly 400 scans from the lead retrieval device. The CoSAI representation was strong, and the use of Legos was successful in encouraging conversations. Daniel suggested leveraging the lead retrieval list to actively push CoSAI content to those who expressed specific interest, a suggestion that Kelly committed to passing to Holly.  
  * *Ernst & Young-hosted Panel:* Sarah and J.R. debriefed on a lunch discussion hosted by Sarah Liang from Ernst & Young, which was also attended by representatives from companies that are currently not involved in CoSAI. The discussion focused on the tension between the grand vision of continuous attestation throughout the full life cycle and the current implementation challenges faced by organizations. Daniel Rohrer noted that the representation from a big e-commerce platform was particularly interesting because they build everything from scratch, making them a valuable perspective for CoSAI to explore.  
  * *Global Outreach and Participation:* David and Daniel emphasized the need to expand CoSAI participation beyond the US to Europe, Asia, and Brazil to avoid becoming an echo chamber. Daniel  noted the low visibility of CoSAI and OASIS in Europe, citing events like OC3, which was focused on sovereign AI. David highlighted the successful engagement at the CoSAI co-sponsored InCyber Forum round table session in France and suggested a focused strategy on key regions with countries like the Netherlands, Italy, and Australia, is needed. Debadatta offered support for scaling efforts in Brazil and Asia.  
  * *EU AI Act Engagement and Future European Event:* Sarah reported that Daniela Taveau (Chair of the OASIS Board of Directors) has connections to the EU Parliament and wants to organize an EU-based event around CoSAI which Sarah will help design. J.R. suggested that CoSAI should put effort into making headway on the EU AI Act this year.  
* **SIG 1 and SIG 2 Lead Status and Meeting Schedule:**   
  * Akila checked on the status of formalizing the SIG 1 and SIG 2 leads, to which Claudia confirmed they are still seeking volunteers to lead the SIGs. Efforts are ongoing to schedule a regular meeting time for SIG 2\.  
  * Akila noted the need to identify additional contributors who are not already involved so that the SIGs are not cannibalizing the rest of the workstreams, suggesting that group members should find the right representatives from their companies to volunteer for SIG 1 and SIG 2\. Claudia reported that SIG 1 has a regular meeting schedule and, despite the lack of officially appointed leads, participation from Cisco and others has made it productive.  
  * Claudia noted that a next meeting for SIG 2 had not yet been scheduled following the two kickoff sessions and the interruption by RSA. Akila suggested taking the scheduling of SIG 2 offline and allowing the SIG members to coordinate the time that works best for them. J.R. suggested that for the next one to two weeks, they should be guided by Ian and Sarah regarding SIG 2, as they are heavily involved and will discuss it in the WS4 slots before advising on priority items and meeting times.  
* **WS4 Update and ADLC/Cookbooks:**   
  * Sarah and Ian detailed the two parallel paths for WS4: further scoping down the Agent Development Life Cycle (ADLC) which is currently part of SIG 2, and developing "cookbooks" (playbooks/runbooks) containing code and execution-based content to accompany the MCP paper from last fall. Daniel suggested that the cookbooks should be closer to executable skills or components to enable workflows, rather than just documentation, to improve adoption.  
  * Daniel advocated for increased cross-workstream collaboration, possibly through building canonical use cases or workflows that stitch together components from WS3 (compliance/control) and WS4. This approach would require infrastructure planning, such as an API backend for AI models. Sarah and Ian agreed that they need more contributors, to help define and secure a single, common use case that spans all work stream areas for demonstration purposes. Sarah asked the TSC members to connect with their company’s developer advocates to attract more participants.  
* **Shared Responsibility Framework Review and Action Institute Briefing:**  
  * Josiah asked for a TSC review and a vote on the Shared Responsibility Framework document, which has been revised in response to comments from David and Jason Garman. J.R. confirmed that he and Akila will review the document as a next step before the full TSC review and vote.  
  * J.R. reminded the group about a planned information interchange with the Action Institute, a university consortium funded by NSF, DHS, and IBM, to align on agentic security work, likely scheduled for April or early May.
