Ingrid announced that Sarah’s blog post recap of her and Jason’s the RSAC presentation was also approved, with publication anticipated later this week and inclusion in the upcoming press release  
* **Discussion on Shared Responsibility Framework (SRF) Paper**  
  *  J.R. initiated a discussion regarding the next steps for the SRF paper, acknowledging the team's difficulty in communicating the broad framework and its importance, comparing it in significance to the CoSAI Risk Map. Bill offered to donate some tools he built to CoSAI to support the paper's application. An email with more detail regarding the tools was sent to the TSC and WS3 mailing lists.  
  * The proposed course of action for the SRF paper involves two steps: advancing the paper to a full majority vote by the TSC, and moving the resulting work to WS3 as a living document. This work would co-reside with the CoSAI risk map work and potentially involve creating a SIG to utilize the tooling Bill is donating.  
  * Karttik supported the proposition, mentioning that WS3’s focus on mapping risks and controls will help operationalize the framework. Josiah agreed, suggesting that WS2 could recruit builders to develop tooling for other CoSAI products, focusing on smaller, actionable chunks of workBill noted that the SRF takes time to explain, and tooling, such as a wizard, is needed to show how it functions as an accountability spine. Lauren inquired about potential overlap between the proposed SRF tooling and WS2’s ongoing telemetry efforts, suggesting a unified approach for best practices.  
  *  J.R. explained that the overall deliverable is the CoSAI Risk Map, with individual workstreams surfacing controls that become part of it. Companion activity, like the SRF, addresses how these controls are made actionable regarding shared responsibilities between providers and consumers.   
* **Formalizing SRF Review and Donation**  
  * Claudia outlined the formal next steps: starting the official review period for the SRF paper with the PGB and TSC simultaneously, followed by a TSC ballot. Regarding the tooling donation, David L. suggested that WS3 should review Bill’s donation first, ensuring they agree to consume and support it before it is surfaced to the TSC.  
  * Zeal stated that WS3 will take over the ownership of the SRF work, including mapping SRF personas to CoSAI-RM personas. J.R. clarified that the paper's publication will occur before the work is transferred to WS3, where Zeal and the other WS3 co-chairs will decide how to integrate Bill’s donated tooling, possibly by spinning up a SIG.  
  *  J.R. emphasized the difficulty of the SRF paper for the audience and stressed the importance of a blog post to communicate its aspects clearly, possibly with definitive examples. Ingrid will connect with Casey, Marketing Committee co-chair to get the blog post drafted.  
* **Workstream Information Sharing Discussion**  
  *  J.R. introduced the topic of improving information sharing and connectivity between the different workstreams and SIGs, which was brought up in past TSC meetings.   
  * David L. elaborated on the challenges of open community collaboration, noting that coordination beyond best effort is complex because each community has its own mission. He confirmed ongoing conversations with various entities, including a follow-up with the UCSB Action Institute, to find areas of collaboration.  
  * Akila brought up the possibility of using the Slack community for communication, but David L. noted that the cost of premium Slack with history retention would be significant, and the topic will be taken back to the PGB for further discussion.  
  * David L. suggested that a CoSAI GitHub Project board, similar to the CoSAI-RM Explorer could serve as a singular, straightforward place to publicize cross-workstream issues and deliverables in a "now, next, later, never" format.   
  * Shrey noted that WS3's GitHub homepage needs improvement to clearly outline current work, provide easy connection points, and better onboard new members. David L. supported the use of GitHub Issues and Discussions for materializing content to facilitate external exposure and agent-based summarization, rather than relying on Google Docs.  
  * Lauren reinforced the need for a single source of truth, proposing that workstream leads ensure their GitHub READMEs are up-to-date for new members and link to any external working documents. Salim expressed a strong need for simple onboarding steps, clarity on who to talk to, and a list of small tasks to get started.  
  * Sarah noted that WS4 is already using AI skills to automatically generate agendas and trying to manage meeting notes in a cleaner way. Sarah suggested that the community should look at all independent efforts and try to establish a more unified CoSAI model for sharing.  
  * David proposed setting up a group of interested volunteers from the workstreams to document gaps, discuss desired sharing levels, and curate a better communication plan. Josiah confirmed that he, Shrey, and Sarah would join this effort, with Jay volunteering to join from WS1, ensuring representation from each workstream.
