### Meeting Notes – Based on AI-generated summary

* **Shared Responsibility Framework Vote and Transition to WS3:**  
  * J.R. reported that the vote for the Shared Responsibility Framework (AI SRF) concluded successfully with 10 votes, and the PGB consensus call will begin this week. Josiah is coordinating the transition of this work to WS3 and will invite interested parties to future scheduling sessions.  
* **Security of AI-Assisted Code SIG Leadership:**   
  * J.R. noted that a non-Cisco co-lead for the Security of AI-Assisted Code SIG is still needed. The committee decided to keep this vacancy on the agenda and may solicit volunteers directly from the current SIG participants.  
* **Global Meeting Logistics Proposal:**   
  * J.R. introduced a proposal to adjust TSC meeting times to better accommodate a global audience, specifically proposing an alternating schedule between Monday at 11:00 a.m. and Tuesday at 9:00 p.m. David L. argued that demonstrating scheduling flexibility is essential to encourage participation from international entities, while Akila emphasized the need to balance this with maintaining consistent attendance from the current membership. The committee agreed to move the proposal to an asynchronous vote to formalize the new schedule.  
* **GitHub-First Operational Architecture:**   
  * J.R. proposed shifting committee operations to a GitHub-first architecture to create a unified source of truth, moving away from fragmented reliance on Google Docs and Slack. The plan includes using GitHub issues and repositories for agenda tracking, deliverable management, and meeting documentation. Several workstreams have already begun experimenting with these structures, and the committee aims to reach a consensus on a standardized approach to improve transparency and accessibility.  
* **Knowledge Management and Artificial Intelligence Tools:**   
  * Akila opened a discussion on evaluating the use of LLMs to solve the committee's knowledge discovery challenge. Josiah recommended building a knowledge graph retrieval-augmented generation system to index GitHub information using a structured representation, while Claudia advised keeping the initial implementation simple to avoid unnecessary complexity. The committee agreed to prioritize the standardization of GitHub workflows first while keeping the potential for future AI-based integrations under consideration.  
* **Action Institute Collaboration:**   
  * J.R. announced upcoming meetings with the Action Institute on June 2nd and June 9th to foster collaboration between industry and academic efforts. Participants will utilize a provided common slide deck to communicate the focus and progress of their respective workstreams and SIGs. Claudia will set up dedicated meeting sessions for these exchanges. The goal of this interaction is to catalyze discussions that may lead to the sharing of prototypes, tools, or future academic representation within the governing body.  
* **Collaboration with Ben-Gurion University:**   
  * J.R. introduced potential collaboration opportunities with Prof. Yisroel Mirsky from BGU regarding CWEs and Common Attack Pattern Enumeration and Classification, as well as the GAVEL project focused on white-box interpretability through activation monitoring. Josiah and Asmae plan to coordinate on the vulnerability classification work, while David L. and J.R. will draft a document to evaluate the feasibility and scope of the GAVEL project to determine its alignment with the committee's charter.  
* **TSC and PGB Leadership Elections:**   
  * David L. reminded the group that August is significant as it marks two years since the last elections for TSC and PGB co-chairs. He advised participants to start considering their interest in these roles and noted that they, along with the OASIS staff, plan to make a formal call for candidates in early July.  
  * David L. also announced a plan to propose to the PGB that the TSC leadership be expanded to a minimum of three co-leads, citing the critical nature of the work currently managed by Akila and J.R. and the need to provide them with additional support.
