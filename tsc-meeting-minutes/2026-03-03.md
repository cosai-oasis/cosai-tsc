### Summary

* **RSA Plans and Meeting on March 23rd:**  
  * The group discussed the PGB & TSC working session scheduled for Monday, March 23rd, and Claudia shared a link for participants to register for the meeting, specifying options for in-person attendance in San Francisco, Raleigh, or remote participation. Registration is required to ensure access to the physical office locations.  
  * David L. and Claudia confirmed the proposed agenda items for the working session had been accepted and reflected those topics for the group. Key agenda points include 30 minutes for relationship building, forward-looking discussions about goals for the remainder of 2026 and 2027, a readout/discussion from WS3 SIG 1, and collaboration discussions with other organizations such as AI Alliance, OWASP, and CSA.  
* **Status of the Agentic Identity Paper (WS4):**   
  * The Agentic Identity paper has been reviewed by Zeal and Asmae, with a hard deadline for this Friday for the authors to incorporate comments from the reviewers.   
* **Review Status of Shared Responsibility Paper (WS2):**   
  * Josiah indicated that the WS2 Shared Responsibility paper is ready for consideration and final review by the TSC. The governance rules require two TSC reviewers before the official review and approval period starts.  
  * David L. volunteered to be one of the two reviewers. Jason accepted the request to serve as the second reviewer.   
* **Summary of WS3 new SIG Kickoff:**   
  * David L. and Akila summarized the SIG 1 kickoff as productive, noting strong attendance from members across various companies. A primary point of confusion for participants was how to prioritize the various deliverables, and Akila expressed a preference for the group to focus on actionable tooling, frameworks, or governance that can be pushed out in the short term.  
  * David L. noted that the group is currently seeking additional co-leads for this SIG to bring in diverse perspectives. The group emphasized that a successful outcome for SIG 1 would involve creating practical guardrails, policies, and baseline governance applicable to engineers' life cycles, focusing on utility and built-in controls. The SIG is also expected to identify control objectives for SIG 2, particularly concerning how agents are securely deployed and ensuring assumptions are clearly stated.  
* **Scheduling of WS4 SIG 2 Kickoff:**   
  * The participants agreed to prioritize the WS4 SIG 2 (Security of Agent Development Lifecycle) kickoff and decided to hold the session next Tuesday, canceling the regular TSC meeting for that week. The tentative time for the two-hour kickoff is 12:00 PM to 2:00 PM East Coast time. Claudia will be setting up the meeting and sending invites to PGB, TSC, and all Workstream participants.  
* **Discussion of Updated CoSAI Strategy and Agent Narrative:**   
  * David L. introduced a new initiative to update the CoSAI strategy, which will involve aligning on a shared agent narrative to demonstrate the importance of CoSAI  and allied security communities in the autonomous work domain. This paper is intended to be usable by various organizations and focuses on the implications and required controls when an agent acts as an autonomous responder to security events. The paper is currently in draft and being reviewed by a small group, with plans for a broader review by the TSC and PGB by the upcoming Friday.  
* **Proposal for AI Assistant Co-Author Tagging in Repositories:**  
  * David L. presented a proposal to use a vendor-neutral co-author tag for AI-assisted contributions in repositories, suggesting the tag \`AI assistant at CoSAI\` for commits and pull requests. He also suggested a mandatory AI disclosure in the \`README\` file of all repositories to be explicit about the use of AI in past work.  
* **Proposal for Sharing Prompts and Agent Definitions:**  
  * A second proposal by David L. suggested establishing a pattern for sharing vendor-neutral prompts and agent definitions within a dedicated \`agents\` and \`prompts\` directory in each repository's \`scripts\` directory. The goal is to share agents (such as a content reviewer agent) across projects, ensuring the definitions are stripped of vendor-specific front matter and schema elements for broad applicability.
