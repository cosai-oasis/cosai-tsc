### Meeting Summary

* **TSC Ballot Participation \- Shared Responsibility Framework**  
  * The TSC discussed the open ballot for the shared responsibility framework, which currently lacks the necessary 8 'yes' votes for approval, having received only 5 valid votes. Claudia reported that the co-chairs had asked to extend the voting deadline and that she had reached out to members individually to address concerns, but participation remained low due to competing priorities reported by several members.  
  * Salim raised questions regarding the eligibility of new members to vote on initiatives. Claudia clarified that all representatives of foundational and premier sponsors who are designated as primary reps to the TSC are eligible to participate in any ballot, regardless of when they joined the initiative.  
  * David L. noted that persistent busyness is a standard challenge, but argued that if voting members cannot find time to review documents, the team may need to reassess the quality of the materials or the urgency of the votes.   
  * Claudia clarified that there are currently 14 voting members on the TSC, defined as representatives from sponsoring organizations who have maintained consistent attendance in accordance with the project rules.  
* **Cross-workstream Coordination Goals**  
  * Akila and David L. discussed the difficulty of scheduling cross-workstream meetings that accommodate all time zones, specifically for participants in Europe and the APAC region. The group reaffirmed that the primary goal for these coordination efforts is to improve transparency, facilitate easy onboarding for new members, and ensure that work products are discoverable for users and LLMs.  
* **Proposed GitHub-First Workflow**  
  * David L. proposed using GitHub issues as the central knowledge-sharing and tracking platform to standardize workflows across the current workstreams. The goal is to move toward an "automation-first" approach that allows for asynchronous work, reducing the need for constant live meetings while preventing the need for workstream leads to reinvent the wheel.  
  * The group discussed the role of various tools, agreeing that Google Docs remains useful for private or initial collaborative drafting, while GitHub should be the primary destination for public-facing work products. The transition to focussing more on GitHub is intended to ensure data is discoverable and to support the project's long-term transparency and governance requirements.  
  * Josiah suggested that the team should prototype the new GitHub-centric workflow in a workstream with multiple active Special Interest Groups to test its effectiveness, noting that WS2 may not be the optimal candidate due to a recent decline in its activity levels.  
  * Participants noted that while GitHub is the intended "source of truth," not all community members are "Git-native". To remain inclusive, the group agreed to explore integrations that allow contributors to interact via email or Slack, with comments automatically posted back to GitHub, ensuring that the work is captured and retained transparently.  
  * Ian and Sarah expressed concerns about "AI slop," where low-quality, AI-generated contributions on GitHub create noise and waste the time of reviewers. The group agreed to establish clear norms and triage processes, requiring that contributors prioritize existing community-defined issues before introducing new, unvetted contributions.  
  * Claudia and David L. clarified that Slack cannot serve as an official business tool for CoSAI because it currently lacks the required retention and transparency mechanisms to comply with the project's open governance rules.  
* **Governance of Artificial Intelligence Usage**  
  * Salim inquired about guidelines for using LLMs with project data. David L. clarified that the current guidance permits the use of any LLM, provided that the project avoids bias toward specific vendors and maintains its neutral standing.  
  * Daniel, Jay and David L. discussed the complexities surrounding Intellectual Property and the use of intermediate, non-published project data in LLMs.   
* **Two-Month New Workflow Experiment**  
  * Akila proposed an 8-week experiment to implement the new GitHub-first workflow for the TSC and WS4.
