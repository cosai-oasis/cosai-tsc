### Meeting Notes

* **Review of Agentic Identity Paper Ballot and Publication**  
  * The administrative item concerning the ballot for the agentic identity paper was discussed, with  Claudia noting that a vote must be done on the mailing list because there were not enough voting members present at the meeting. Voting members were asked to go ahead and cast their vote using the link provided in the mailing list or by sending their response via reply to the mailing list.  
  * The official five-day review period for the agentic identity paper concluded the previous Sunday, with Zeal and Asmae serving as the official reviewers. Co-authors addressed submitted comments, which were mostly minor, such as linking to the MCP security paper and adding items to the appendix.  
  * Though the initial goal was to publish the agentic identity paper before the RSAC conference next week, Claudia clarified that a successful vote allows publishing a draft approved by the TSC, the fully approved version cannot be published until the PGB votes on it.  
  * Ian agreed to prepare a PDF draft of the paper with a "draft" watermark.  
* **SIG 2 Kickoff and Scope**  
  * Ian provided an update on SIG 2, Security of Agent Development Lifecycle,  
    which recently held its second follow-up session, detailing that the scope includes security, governance, sandboxing policies, and admission throughout the agent lifecycle. The team is allowing two more weeks for asynchronous review and comment collection, and they are seeking one or two volunteers to step up as SIG leads, acknowledging that the topic is extremely broad. Out-of-scope items for SIG 2 explicitly include compliance, privacy, differential privacy, standard safety concerns, and marketplaces, while automated code generation is out of scope and leveraged from SIG 1\.  
* **Interoperability and Focus of SIG 1 and SIG 2**  
  * SIG 1 is focused on code and coding security, while SIG 2 addresses the infrastructure for agent development, including isolation, integrity, and attestations. Sarah confirmed that SIG 1 is attached to WS3 and SIG 2 to WS4. Shrey suggested finding synergies and collaboration opportunities between SIG 1 and SIG 2 to ensure no critical topics are removed from the overall scope of CoSAI.  
  * Akila suggested having SIG 1 and SIG 2 maintain a rolling list of in-scope and out-of-scope items and regularly revisit the out-of-scope items to prevent duplication of efforts or missed work. Ian and Sarah noted difficulty in remotely tagging or assigning issues to SIG owners in GitHub due to organizational membership issues, which Claudia offered to help resolve.  
* **Governance of Agentic Coding Systems**   
  * Jason raised a tactical question regarding the governance of agentic coding systems (e.g., Cursor, Claude Code), specifically asking if recommended observability, action tracing, and access control for these systems are within the scope of any current SIGs or papers. Ian pointed out that SIG 1 does mention governance within its paper, and the Agentic Futures paper addresses autonomous agents by advocating for a human-in-the-loop review process.  
* **Scope of SIG 1 and Need for Diverse Leadership**  
  * David clarified that the scope of SIG 1 is intended to be broader than just the initial CodeGuard donation, with plans to focus on aspects like threat modeling. He emphasized the need for more than one co-lead in SIG 1 to ensure a broader focus beyond the current Cisco representation and encouraged Jason to contribute his insights on governance to the group.  
* **Agentic Futures Paper Overview**  
  * David provided an overview of the agentic futures paper, which aims to move the community toward a shared understanding of where Agentic AI might be going, beyond single-agent models, to address collaboration between human and non-human entities. The paper is intended as a thought piece, identifying control and security problems relevant to the domain and setting a shared definition of agentic AI for 2026\.  
  * Akila asked all participants to review the agentic futures paper and file any strong feedback as a pull request, emphasizing the importance of reading it before the RSA conference to enable further discussions. David accepted responsibility for fixing issues with the PDF document.  
* **Discussion on Workstream Restructuring**  
  * David introduced the idea of discussing whether the current workstream structure still serves the group's needs, given the new agentic futures paper and the creation of SIGs. Bill suggested that a topic hierarchy could help map out workstream interactions and identify gaps. Akila agreed that constant review of the workstream structure is important and that the maturity of SIG 1 and SIG 2 may define future reshaping.  
* **Meeting Schedule and Other Business**   
  * The group agreed to cancel the TSC meeting scheduled for the following week due to the RSAC conference.
