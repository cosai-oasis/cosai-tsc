## Summary

* **Administrative Matters and RSAC Preparations:**   
  * The discussion moved to administrative matters, specifically upcoming activities around the RSAC conference. Claudia highlighted that an email with a "save the date" for all CoSAI-related sessions had been sent, and they noted a joint TSC PGB co-working session planned for Monday, March 23rd, at 11 a.m. PT at Cisco’s offices or remotely. Claudia is awaiting confirmation on the agenda for this session from the PGB and TSC co-chairs.  
  * In addition to the TSC/PGB session, other CoSAI related events at RSAC were confirmed, including a breakfast panel at Anthropic on Tuesday morning and a lunch round table at EY’s offices on Wednesday. Sarah and Jason are also scheduled to present on the MCP security paper during the main program on Wednesday, and J.R. and Akila have a session on Thursday. Registration is required for some of these events.  
  * J.R. requested that all RSAC event details be compiled in one easy-to-access location for the TSC. Claudia confirmed that she would add a document to the TSC drive with the information, which Ingrid noted is also partially available on the [CoSAI website](https://www.coalitionforsecureai.org/cosai-at-rsac-2026/) for the public events. Akila agreed that a centralized calendar or similar resource would be helpful for the group, as many members have packed agendas that week.  
  * Akila reported that she and J.R. had received feedback on their RSAC presentation from the review committee and plan to incorporate it. Akila offered to share a draft with the group and also extended an offer to help Sarah and Jason with their MCP security paper presentation.  
* **WS1 Updates and Need for New Co-leads:** 
  * WS1 is undergoing co-lead changes as Andre is stepping down, and Matt will be taking parental leave. The team is actively seeking new co-leads to replace them. Jay will continue as one of the co-chairs for the working group.  
  * Jay provided an update on WS1, stating that they are currently focused on completing their next document push, including whittling down content and delegating sections to contributors. They are also collaborating with the AI and ML Security Working Group in the OpenSSF on common efforts, including the topic of model vulnerabilities. Jay views the current moment as an excellent opportunity for new people to volunteer and contribute to the work on security and vulnerability management for models.  
  * When asked about the vision for WS1, Jay explained that their thought process is macro, focusing on collaborating across other internal workstreams (SIG 1 and WS3) and external organizations. The primary goal is to prevent duplication of efforts and to ensure the industry addresses shared problems collectively, as these issues will impact everyone’s mutual customers.  
  * J.R. reiterated the call for interested individuals to nominate themselves as co-leads for WS1 by contacting either Akila or himself.   
* **WS2 Shared Responsibility Model Review Process:**  
  * Vinay reported that the [Shared Responsibility Model paper](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1RhLM_CGkklKjwGTWq9GIHAj_ZLenjRoqoiYFnHpzsUo/edit?usp=sharing) for WS2 is ready for TSC review and a vote, as feedback from the TSC has been incorporated. Claudia suggested a two-step process: first, an unofficial review period for final comments, followed by the official 5-day review period for the PGB and TSC. J.R. confirmed that the plan is to allow the TSC members one week for final comments before advancing the paper to a full majority vote.  
* **WS2 Telemetry and Zero Trust Updates:**   
  * Vinay reported that the Telemetry SIG is in the initial stage, with a lot of new interest. They are working to define the scope, which focuses on enhancing existing open standards like OCSF with AI-specific telemetry or logging requirements. For the Zero Trust paper, Jason noted that they have a working session planned for the following day and are working toward getting the paper out for TSC review in the near future.  
  * Jason confirmed that aligning the Zero Trust paper with the CoSAI-RM work, including the recently laid out personas, has been an explicit design goal in recent edits.  
* **WS2 Looking Ahead for New SIGs:**   
  * With the Shared Responsibility Model paper nearing completion, Vinay announced that WS2 will be looking to start a new SIG, and they will discuss potential topics based on available RFIs and the TSC’s backlog in the next 1-2 weeks.  
* **WS3 CoSAI-RM Progress and SIG Launch:**   
  * Shrey reported significant progress on the CoSAI-RM side, including the publication of the [initial set of personas to the main branch for review](https://github.com/cosai-oasis/secure-ai-tooling/blob/main/risk-map/tables/personas-summary.md) and feedback. For SIG Security of AI-assisted Development, a “no objection” vote was cleared last week, and the group needs to move to the next stage of launching the SIG and identifying co-leads.  
  * Christina emphasized the need for co-leads for the new SIG, asking interested parties to reach out. J.R. stressed the urgency of launching SIG 1 and noted the potential for interest from Cisco, given their donation of the Code Guard code. Aaron volunteered themself and Thomas Bartlett as co-leads for the SIG.  
  * David L. thanked the initial volunteers and suggested planning the SIG launch as soon as possible, ideally next week, and asked TSC members to ensure interested parties in their organizations are aware of the meeting date. David L. suggested picking a tentative date, such as Wednesday next week, for the kickoff, allowing the co-leads to be finalized between now and then. Claudia  offered to work with Aaron to set up the calendar invite and add all initial participants from the previous meeting.  
  * David L. strongly encouraged other workstreams to lean in and provide feedback on the CoSAI-RM personas and control sets. He stated that the best way to do this is by submitting GitHub issues on what is missing, wrong, or needs modification, as the current structure is a "good enough" foundation that requires community input to improve.  
* **WS4 Agent Identity Paper Status:**   
  * J.R. asked Ian and Sarah for an update on the Agent Identity paper, which was believed to be nearing readiness for TSC editor review. Raghuram noted that good feedback was received on February 20th and that the team is working on dispositioning those comments to prepare the paper for review. Ian agreed to push the team for an update on the agreed-upon timeline, which aimed for a general vote around the current day, and David L. requested a stable standalone draft document for people to start reviewing.  
* **WS4 Alignment with Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF):**  
  *  J.R. introduced the topic of the Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF) under the Linux Foundation, noting its overlap in membership and mandate with WS4, particularly around working groups on identity, trust, observability, and security. The PGB Executive Steering Committee discussed how CoSAI should align with and complement AAIF's work.  
  * David L. and J.R. said that they would work on a position paper or framing pattern to clarify CoSAI's value proposition regarding agent security challenges, without adding a new work item for WS4 at this time. The goal is to establish a shared framework for thinking about agent usage that highlights critical security problems, such as entitlements, sandboxing, and observability, to foster alignment across organizations like AAIF and OpenSSF and prevent duplication of effort.  
  * Raghuram expressed concern that AAIF might define their own personas and controls, leading to market confusion. David L. responded that deviation is expected in open communities and that CoSAI’s work is structured to be flexible and usable, with mechanisms like mapping to external standards. Jay added that CoSAI should proactively present its existing work (taxonomies and personas) to newer groups like AAIF to encourage standardization and collaboration over siloing.
