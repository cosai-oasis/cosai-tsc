### Meeting Summary

* **Shared Responsibility Framework Ballot and Voting Deadline:**   
  * Claudia asked all attendees to review the ballot posted earlier that morning for the approval of the shared responsibility framework. Only voting members are permitted to participate in the ballot, which remains open until the end of the day on Friday. The PGB call for consensus ballot will follow, pending TSC approval. A blog post to accompany the framework is being handled by the marketing committee.  
  * Given the complexity of the Shared Responsibility Framework (SRF), the blog post will require iteration to ensure the motivating use cases and scenarios are clearly communicated. The plan is to get through the two votes before focusing on the content of the upcoming blog.  
* **Discussion on SIG Co-Lead Participation in TSC Meetings:**   
  * The TSC discussed whether to include SIG co-leads in the meetings, as they have not historically invited all leadership from the older SIGs. Currently, active SIGs include Telemetry and Zero Trust in WS2, with Zero Trust winding down post-publication,  the new WS3 Secure AI-Assisted Code SIG, CoSAI-RM SIG and SecureADLC SIG.  
  * The general sentiment was positive toward inviting SIG co-leads to improve cross-talk and representation. However, concerns were raised about maintaining good TSC membership representation and managing TSC-only business.  
  * Lauren suggested periodically having less frequent meetings where all SIGs join, or only having a subset join each week to avoid imposing too much on everyone's calendars. This approach aims to balance the need for more in-depth context from SIGs with the goal of fostering cross-collaboration.  
* **Addressing Time Zone Awareness and Meeting Cadence:**   
  * David L. suggested implementing an alternating schedule to enable non-EDT participants to join. He further proposed alternating the type of meeting—having some for TSC-only business and others that include the broader group of Workstream and SIG leads.  
  * The consensus was that if meetings are rescheduled, a comprehensive, one-time change should be implemented rather than incremental changes. The proposed alternating schedule is seen as a good plan that would help with cross-SIG detail and address time zone limitations for global participation.  
* **Initiative to Systematize Deliverables and Meeting Agendas:**  
  *  J.R. and Akila are attempting to raise the level of organization by using the TSC GitHub repository to actively track deliverables and meeting agendas. They created a new branch with a 'TSC deliverables' folder to track items like papers, blogs, and tools, complete with templates for proposing new deliverables and tracking their progress against target dates.  
  * The suggestion is to crowdsource meeting agendas, using the TSC repository to allow all participants to open issues and suggest agenda items, thereby simplifying the planning process. The workflow would involve creating a weekly meeting file and pointing it to the transcripts folder to track action items and follow-ups.  
  * Sarah and Ian explained that they have been using the discussions format for WS4's agendas, aided by an AI tool that reads open pull requests and meeting minutes to generate action items and follow-ups. This approach aims for more asynchronous interaction and improved tracking of resolutions.  
  * A discussion arose because several groups, including David L. for the CoSAI-RM SIG and the WS4 co-leads, are developing parallel project management solutions. David L. expressed a desire to merge these efforts, as they share the same goals of standardizing processes across workstreams.  
  * J.R. suggested expanding the scope of David Ls's cross-workstream discussion to include the operational plumbing and systematization work presented by the WS4 co-leads and himself. Asmae supported this, noting that templates and guidance on managing agenda items and expectations would be very helpful for new co-leads.  
* **Debate on LLM Selection for Agentic Tools:**   
  * David L. raised concerns about the implicit selection of an LLM for agentic tools, arguing that choosing a singular platform could be seen as disparaging members who offer competing services. The consensus was that the tools should remain vendor neutral and that the output should be published as “AI-assisted from CoSAI” without constraining which LLM is used.  
  * Akila stated that LLMs are a means to the end of better coordination and situational awareness for all members, emphasizing that the workstreams should use whatever model effectively integrates with tools like Google Drive, GitHub, and Slack. Daniel and other participants supported the idea that models should be in service of the goal, and that choice of models should be allowed for different use cases.  
* **Confirmation of Follow-up Meeting on Organizational Strategy:**   
  * J.R. summarized the discussion, confirming that the current plan is to have a group, including David L. and Asmae, convene to discuss and propose how best to organize and to what extent AI automation will be used. David L. confirmed they will bring this topic up during the first instance of their cross-workstream discussion meeting, tentatively scheduled for Friday.  
* **Upcoming Action Institute Engagement Dates**  
  * J.R. announced that CoSAI has secured two dates with the Action Institute, a premier university-led security group funded by the NSF, DHS, and IBM. On June 2nd, CoSAI workstream leads will present their work and deliverables for one hour, followed by the Action Institute PIs presenting their work on June 9th, with a discussion on future engagement.  
  * Josiah requested a link to a shared slide deck where workstream leads can contribute content for the June 2nd presentation to the Action Institute. J.R. confirmed that he would work with Claudia to create an outline and provide the necessary resources with four weeks lead time.  
* **Sharing the Draft AI Usage Policy:**  
  * Ian and Sarah shared a link to their draft AI Usage Policy, which they created in response to observing random "drive by AI pull requests". They presented it as a starting point for the TSC and governing board to consider as they finalize the official policy.
