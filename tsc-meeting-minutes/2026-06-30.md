* **WS2 Telemetry Proposal and Persona Vectors:**   
  * Josiah introduced a proposal to develop a telemetry standard that exposes a projection of model activation states to test for alignment. The group discussed the technical feasibility, security utility, and scope of using "persona vectors" for this purpose.  
  * David L. and J.R. expressed concerns regarding whether the proposed telemetry work falls within the security mission. They emphasized the need for a compelling security use case and clear evidence of defender advantage to justify the effort, noting that mechanistic interpretability is currently outside the primary scope.  
  * J.R. noted that earlier work with Ben-Gurion University on Project Gavel explored similar mechanistic interpretability concepts. The group agreed that any further development in this area must clearly bridge the gap between internal model activation states and practical security implications.  
  * Josiah explained that the telemetry group is working with the Open Cybersecurity Schema Framework (OCSF) to extend schemas for AI telemetry. They plan to release a white paper and accompanying blog posts to illustrate specific attack scenarios and the current state of AI telemetry.  
  * Ian added that there are discussions with OCSF contributors regarding agentic and AI-specific schema additions. This collaboration aims to improve provenance information for making informed access control decisions.   
* **WS3 Updates:**  
  * Shrey reported the release of bug fixes for the CodeGuard plugin and ongoing benchmarking activities and that infrastructure work for CoSAI-RM version 1 is largely complete.  
  * Christina and David L. discussed the challenge of mapping AI personas to security controls. They aim to achieve better alignment by creating more precise personas for governance, risk, and compliance responsibilities, acknowledging that this requires overcoming sector-specific complexities.  
  * David L. described an initiative to decompose existing papers into a ledger of components. This effort is intended to facilitate manageable review processes and involves identifying and adding new categories related to primary identity and agent infrastructure.  
  * Karttik provided an update on a plugin project designed to operationalize the SRF, noting they are currently testing the tool and evaluating hosting options for the project.  
* **WS4 MCP Security Specification:**  
  * Ian reported on the progress of the MCP Security paper, version 2\. The team is working toward a hard publication deadline of July 28th to ensure the documentation coincides with the release of the specification.  
  * Claudia confirmed that a version 2 release with substantial additions requires a formal review and vote by the TSC and the PGB. The group agreed to provide a diff of the changes to facilitate this review.  
  * *Coordination with other AI Security Groups:* Participants discussed overlapping meeting times and missions with a related AI security group. It was agreed to seek a formal bridge between the organizations to clarify their respective roles and identify potential collaboration opportunities.  
    * Jay discussed the goal of streamlining artifacts by leveraging existing work within the ecosystem rather than duplicating efforts. Jay expressed concern that a Working Group outside of CoSAI is currently creating redundant content regarding agentic topics. David L. suggested that all members who attend the meetings of the other group, which currently overlap with the CoSAI TSC meetings, should coordinate to develop a strategy for engagement. David L. plans to contact the Working Group co-lead to push for better alignment.
